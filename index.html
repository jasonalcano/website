Jason C. Alcano

I am graduate student from Taipei Medical University in Taipei, Taiwan (R.O.C.). I mainly work applications of mass spectrometry in pharmaceutical sciences such as in natural products chemistry (plants and marine resources) and clinical metabolomics analysis.

CV Google Scholar ResearchGate

Here are the research interests I work on

Mass Spectrometry Metabolomics

Application of gas chromatography and liquid chromatography coupled to a triple quadrupole (TQ) or a quadrupole time-of-flight (QTOF) MS systems for qualitative and quantitative workflows

Natural Products Bioprospecting

I study plant and marine secondary metabolites and their bioctivities using multiple spectroscopic techniques and bioinformatics tools such MZmine, molecular networking and FBMN-stats

Biomarker and Biomedical Analysis

Analyzes human biological fluids for potential diagnostic or prognostic markers using univariable and multivariable statistical methods

My interests mainly revolved on the most downstream read-out of the omics technologies, metabolomics. I am also starting to gather interest in proteomics and glycoproteomics analysis (but still currently learning the methodologies)
